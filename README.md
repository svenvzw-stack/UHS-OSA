# UHS-OSA
Universal Holistic System – Open Structural Architecture
## What is UHS-OSA?
UHS-OSA defines a technology-independent structural grammar for organizing operational information and tools across organizations, systems and platforms.
It enables consistent structures across:
- documents
- folder structures
- portals and navigators
- digital systems and AI-based services

The purpose is to improve interoperability, usability and long-term stability independent of technology.
UHS-OSA is intended to function as a common structural reference comparable to classification systems such as DDC/UDC for libraries, but for operational environments.
## Open Core
The UHS-OSA Open Core is freely available and may be implemented without prior approval.
The Open Core defines the invariant structural grammar through:
- Operational Decimal Classification (ODC) levels 1–4
- structural models and perspective logic
- principles for portals, folders and navigation

For the Operation Manual (OMA), structural invariance extends to levels 5–6 to ensure sufficient granularity for governance, navigation and operational consistency.
The Open Core defines how the structure works, not all possible content within the structure.
See LICENSE for formal terms.

## Extended Materials
UHS distinguishes between the Open Core and Extended Materials.
Extended Materials include:
- detailed classification structures
- templates and reference implementations
- governance, certification and ecosystem services
Extended Materials are managed through a stewardship and licensing model.
## Scope of Publication
This repository contains:
- the Open Core of UHS-OSA
- selected materials necessary for understanding and implementation
Additional materials, including full extensions and governance frameworks, are managed under a structured stewardship model and are not part of this public release.
## Interaction Model
The Open Core may be implemented freely without any contact with the Originator.
The Originator does not provide:
- implementation support
- advisory services
- commercial discussions
All such interactions are handled exclusively through the designated steward or authorized partner.
## Stewardship Opportunity
UHS is designed as a long-term, globally applicable structural framework requiring independent stewardship.
A steward is responsible for:
- governance and evolution of the framework
- management of extended materials
- licensing and certification
- ecosystem development and coordination
Organizations with the capability to operate this role at scale are invited to express interest.
Detailed materials regarding governance, licensing and stewardship are available upon request.
